    Scientific publications from ASAP

    ASAP aims to publish preprints to ensure our scientific advances are rapidly and openly available.

      An in-solution snapshot of SARS-COV-2 main protease maturation process and inhibition

      Gabriela Dias Noske, Yun Song, Rafaela Sachetto Fernandes, Rod Chalk, Haitem Elmassoudi, Lizbé Koekemoer, C. David Owen, Tarick J. El-Baba, Carol V. Robinson, The COVID Moonshot Consortium, Glaucius Oliva, Andre Schutzer Godoy
      Preprint ahead of publication: [bioRxiv]
      Published: Nature Communications 14:1545, 2023. [DOI] | PMC10027274
      Data accompanying the publication: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-023-37035-5#data-availability
      Contributing Projects and Cores: Project 2
      A 3.5A cryo-EM structure of the SARS-CoV-2 main viral protease (Mpro) provides critical details of the N-terminal cleavage event and how different antivirals shift populations toward monomer or dimer.

      Fitness effects of mutations to SARS-CoV-2 proteins

      Jesse D. Bloom, Richard A. Neher
      Preprint ahead of publication: [bioRxiv] | CC-BY 4.0
      Code accompanying the publication: jbloomlab/SARS2-mut-fitness
      Data accompanying the publication: https://jbloomlab.github.io/SARS2-mut-fitness/
      Contributing Projects and Cores: Project 1
      In this work, we present an analysis of SARS-CoV-2 circulating variants to develop a detailed map of functional tolerance to mutations of all SARS-CoV-2 target proteins to inform the discovery of antivirals that avoid intrinsic resistance.

      Turning high-throughput structural biology into predictive inhibitor design

      Kadi L. Saar, William McCorkindale, Daren Fearon, Melissa Boby, Haim Barr, Amir Ben-Schmuel, The COVID Moonshot Consortium, Nir London, Frank von Delft, John D. Chodera, Alpha A. Lee
      Preprint ahead of publication: [bioRxiv]
      Published: Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences USA 120:e2214168120, 2023. [DOI] | PMC10089178
      Code accompanying the publication: kadiliissaar/ligand_design_structural_biology
      Contributing Projects and Cores: Project 3 Biochemical Assay Core Structural Biology Core
      We develop a machine learning framework that leverages high-throughput X-ray crystallography data to outperform ligand- or structure-based methods in predicting potent ligands, and use this approach to discover a noncovalent nonpeptidomimetic inhibitor with 120 nM antiviral efficacy.

      Accelerating antiviral drug discovery: lessons from COVID-19

      Annette von Delft, Matthew D. Hall, Ann Kwong, Lisa A. Purcell, Kumar Singh Saikatendu, Uli Schmitz, John A. Tallarico, and Alpha Lee
      Published: Nature Reviews Drug Discovery in press. [DOI]
      Contributing Projects and Cores: Project 6
      We present a broad industry and academic perspective on key stages in the small-molecule antiviral discovery process and present strategies that could accelerate future efforts.

      Allosteric regulation and crystallographic fragment screening of SARS-CoV-2 NSP15 endoribonuclease

      Andre Schutzer Godoy, Aline Minalli Nakamura, Alice Douangamath, Yun Song, Gabriela Dias Noske, Victor Oliveira Gawriljuk, Rafaela Sachetto Fernandes, Humberto D Muniz Pereira, Ketllyn Irene Zagato Oliveira, Daren Fearon, Alexandre Dias, Tobias Krojer, Michael Fairhead, Alisa Powell, Louise Dunnet, Jose Brandao-Neto, Rachael Skyner, Rod Chalk, Dávid Bajusz, Miklós Bege, Anikó Borbás, György Miklós Keserű, Frank von Delft, Glaucius Oliva
      Published: Nucleic Acids Research in press. [DOI]
      Data accompanying the publication: https://fragalysis.diamond.ac.uk/viewer/react/preview/target/NSP15_B
      Contributing Projects and Cores: Project 2 Structural Biology Core
      We conducted a large fragment screening campaign against SARS-CoV-2 nsp15 endoribonuclease and identified several new allosteric sites that could be targeted for the development of new inhibitors.

      Open science discovery of potent noncovalent SARS-CoV-2 main protease inhibitors

      Melissa L. Boby, Daren Fearon, Matteo Ferla, Mihajlo Filep, Lizbé Koekemoer, Matthew C. Robinson, The COVID Moonshot Consortium, John D. Chodera, Alpha A. Lee, Nir London, Annette von Delft, Frank von Delft
      Preprint ahead of publication: [bioRxiv]
      Published: Science 382:eabo7201, 2023. [DOI]
      Data accompanying the publication: https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abo7201
      We describe the open science discovery of a potent noncovalent SARS-CoV-2 main protease inhibitor, which was discovered through a combination of high-throughput crystallography, machine learning, and synthetic chemistry.
